
Amitabh Bachchan

Amitabh Bachchan, born on October 11, 1942, in Allahabad, India, is one of the most iconic and influential actors in the history of Indian cinema. Known as the "Shahenshah of Bollywood," he gained popularity in the early 1970s with movies like "Zanjeer," "Deewaar," and "Sholay," earning him the nickname "Angry Young Man" of Hindi cinema.

Over the decades, Bachchan has appeared in over 200 Indian films in a career spanning more than five decades. His performances are noted for their intensity, versatility, and commanding screen presence. He has received numerous accolades, including four National Film Awards as Best Actor, the Dadasaheb Phalke Award, and many international honors.

Beyond acting, Amitabh Bachchan has worked as a playback singer, film producer, and television host, most notably of the popular quiz show "Kaun Banega Crorepati." His deep baritone voice and charismatic persona have made him a beloved figure across generations.

In addition to his cinematic achievements, he has also been involved in various humanitarian causes and served as a goodwill ambassador for UNICEF's polio eradication campaign. Despite facing several setbacks, including financial troubles and health issues, Bachchan has consistently reinvented himself and remains an enduring symbol of resilience and excellence in Indian culture.

Amitabh Bachchan's legacy continues to inspire millions, and he is widely regarded as one of the greatest actors the world has ever seen.
